# ASTRA-X: Autonomous Defence Readiness Intelligence

## 1. The Problem Identified
Modern defense logistics and autonomous fleets (such as supply trucks and reconnaissance drones) generate massive, continuous streams of telemetry data. 
Currently, managing this fleet is incredibly difficult:
- **Reactive vs. Proactive:** Maintenance and supply restocking are often reactive, leading to asset downtime in critical moments.
- **Data Overload:** Human operators cannot monitor thousands of data points per second to identify subtle anomalies (like a cyber attack or gradual engine failure).
- **High-Stakes Decision Making:** When an anomaly is detected, shutting down or rerouting an asset manually takes too long. However, letting AI autonomously shut down defense assets is extremely dangerous—if the AI is hacked, hallucinates, or acts maliciously, it could sabotage operations.

## 2. The Solution
**ASTRA-X** is an AI-driven, multi-agent platform designed to automate defense readiness and fleet management. It continuously monitors synthetic telemetry data from assets and uses specialized Machine Learning models to predict needs.

Instead of human operators making every micro-decision, ASTRA-X employs a **LangGraph-powered AI Agent Workflow**:
1. **Inventory Agent:** Checks supply predictions to decide if a restock is needed.
2. **Maintenance Agent:** Checks failure probabilities to schedule repairs.
3. **Risk Agent:** Checks anomaly scores to identify immediate threats.
4. **Command Agent:** Synthesizes the recommendations of the other three agents to make a final tactical decision (e.g., `continue_operation`, `monitor`, `schedule_maintenance`, `freeze_operation`).

## 3. Why These Specific ML Models?
ASTRA-X uses an ensemble of distinct models because different data problems require different mathematical approaches:

*   **LightGBM (Inventory Prediction):** 
    LightGBM is a highly efficient gradient boosting framework. It handles regression tasks (predicting a continuous number, like "daily fuel usage drops") exceptionally well and trains extremely fast, making it ideal for tracking depleting resources across large fleets.
*   **XGBoost (Maintenance Prediction):** 
    XGBoost is the industry gold-standard for classification tasks on tabular data. It is highly accurate at identifying nonlinear patterns in engine stress, temperature, and operating hours to classify whether an asset has a high "Failure Probability."
*   **Isolation Forest (Risk / Anomaly Detection):** 
    Unlike maintenance, you rarely have historical data of every possible cyber attack or physical sabotage. Isolation Forest is an *unsupervised* algorithm specifically designed for anomaly detection. It doesn't need to know what a threat looks like; it simply isolates data points that fall outside the "normal" clusters, allowing the Risk Agent to detect zero-day anomalies.

## 4. Why Terminal 3?
The core risk of ASTRA-X is that an autonomous AI agent has the power to issue a `freeze_operation` command to a defense asset. If an adversary tricks the AI, or the AI hallucinates, it could paralyze a fleet.

**Terminal 3 Network (T3N)** solves this "Zero-Trust AI" problem. Terminal 3 operates using decentralized identities (DIDs) and Trusted Execution Environments (TEEs):
- **Agent Identity:** The AI agent is assigned an Ethereum-based identity. 
- **Tamper-Proof Authorization:** Before the Command Agent is allowed to actually execute a `freeze_operation` on a drone, it must securely handshake with the Terminal 3 bridge. 
- **TEE Contracts:** Terminal 3 checks a tamper-proof WebAssembly (WASM) contract in a secure enclave to verify that this specific agent is allowed to perform this specific action on this specific asset. 
- **The Result:** Even if the AI goes rogue, Terminal 3's cryptographic policies will block the action, ensuring humans and mathematical security protocols maintain ultimate control over the AI.
